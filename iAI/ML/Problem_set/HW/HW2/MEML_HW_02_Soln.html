Problem 1¶

Find solution $D$ of the optimization problem with the following objective function and constraints. Here, $\lVert \cdot \rVert_2$ is the Euclidean norm (or distance).

(Hint: you do not have to find the optimal value of $Z$)



$$\begin{align*} D= \min_Z \quad & \lVert X_1 - Z \rVert_2 + \lVert X_2 - Z \rVert_2 \\ \\ \text{subject to} \quad & Z \in V=\text{span} \{V_1, V_2\} \end{align*}$$

Solution¶

$D^*$ is the shortest distance from $X_1$ to $X_2$ via $Z$ on the spanned hyperplane. The shortest line is the straight line from $X_1$ and $Y$ which is mirrored reflection of $X_2$ with respect to the hyperplane.


$$ \begin{align*} D^* &= \lVert X_1 - MX_2 \rVert = \lVert X_1 - \left( 2V \left(V^TV \right)^{-1}V^T-I\right)X_2\rVert \\ &=\lVert MX_1 - X_2\rVert \end{align*} $$


where $M$ is mirror transformation and $V = [V_1 \; V_2]$
